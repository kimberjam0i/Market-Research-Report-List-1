<p><p>The traction transformer market for locomotives is divided into two primary types: Alternating Current (AC) systems and Direct Current (DC) systems. AC systems utilize transformers to convert high-voltage electricity into a lower voltage for traction motors, providing efficiency and longer transmission distances. In contrast, DC systems directly supply power to traction motors, often preferred for shorter routes and simpler infrastructure. Each system has unique advantages, influencing their applications in various rail networks and impacting overall operational efficiency.</p></p>
