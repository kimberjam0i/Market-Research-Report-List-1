<p><p>Wearable bio sensors for healthcare monitoring are categorized into single-use and repeated-use markets. Single-use sensors are designed for one-time applications, often used in urgent care scenarios or patient monitoring during hospital stays. These are typically lightweight, cost-effective, and ideal for quick assessments. In contrast, repeated-use sensors are built for long-term monitoring, allowing continuous data collection over time. They are often more durable and equipped with advanced features for ongoing health tracking, appealing to consumers seeking chronic condition management.</p></p>
