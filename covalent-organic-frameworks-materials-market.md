<p><p>Covalent Organic Frameworks (COFs) are classified into two primary market types: two-dimensional (2D) and three-dimensional (3D) materials. 2D COFs consist of flat, layered structures that maximize surface area and facilitate rapid adsorption, making them suitable for applications like gas storage and catalysis. In contrast, 3D COFs feature interconnected frameworks that provide enhanced stability and porosity, ideal for environmental remediation and sensing applications. Both types offer unique properties that drive innovation in various fields, including materials science and nanotechnology.</p></p>
