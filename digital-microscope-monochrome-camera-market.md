<p><ul><li>CMOS</li><li>CCD</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The digital microscope monochrome camera market primarily features two sensor types: CMOS and CCD. CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) sensors offer faster image processing, lower power consumption, and cost-effectiveness, making them suitable for various applications. In contrast, CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) sensors are known for their superior image quality and low noise levels, ideal for high-resolution imaging in scientific and industrial settings. The choice between CMOS and CCD depends on the specific imaging requirements, including sensitivity and dynamic range.</p></p>
