<p><p>The 3D hip and knee implant market comprises three primary material types: metal, ceramic, and polyethylene. Metal implants, often made from titanium or cobalt-chromium alloys, provide strength and durability, making them suitable for weight-bearing applications. Ceramic implants offer superior wear resistance and a smooth surface, reducing friction and promoting longevity. Polyethylene is frequently used as a bearing surface in joint replacements, known for its cushioning properties and versatility. Each material type presents unique advantages tailored to patient needs and surgeon preferences.</p></p>
