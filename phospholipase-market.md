<p><p>The phospholipase market is segmented into four main types based on enzyme specificity: Phospholipase A (PLA) hydrolyzes fatty acids from phospholipids, widely used in food and pharmaceuticals. Phospholipase B (PLB) acts on both acyl groups, relevant in lipid metabolism research. Phospholipase C (PLC) cleaves phospholipids to produce diacylglycerol and inositol phosphates, crucial in cell signaling. Phospholipase D (PLD) generates phosphatidic acid, important in lipid modification and industrial applications. Each type serves distinct roles in biotechnology, healthcare, and food industries.</p></p>
