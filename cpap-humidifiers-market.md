<p><p>The CPAP humidifiers market is categorized into temperature-controlled and other types. Temperature-controlled humidifiers actively manage the warmth of the air delivered, enhancing comfort and reducing condensation in the mask. This technology helps maintain optimal humidity levels, improving patient compliance. Conversely, other types may use standard humidity settings without active temperature regulation. These may be simpler or more cost-effective but could lead to varying levels of comfort and potential moisture-related issues during CPAP therapy.</p></p>
