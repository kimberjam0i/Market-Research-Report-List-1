<p><p>The biological catalyst market encompasses six main enzyme types with distinct functions. Oxidoreductases facilitate oxidation-reduction reactions, while transferases transfer functional groups between molecules. Hydrolases catalyze the breakdown of compounds through hydrolysis, and isomerases convert molecules into their isomers. Lyases participate in breaking chemical bonds without water, whereas ligases join molecules together, often using ATP. This diverse range of enzymes plays crucial roles across various industries, including pharmaceuticals, food, and biofuels, driving innovation and market growth.</p></p>
